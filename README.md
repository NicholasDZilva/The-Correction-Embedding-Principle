## Overview

The Correction Embedding Principle (CEP) proposes a structural reframing of AI alignment failure.

Instead of treating misalignment as a deviation from intended behaviour, CEP models it as a **predictable outcome of correct optimisation within structurally incomplete and incentive-constrained objective frames**.

In this framework, failure arises not from incorrect learning, but from:

- Incentive compression over short time horizons  
- Accumulation of irreversible system state changes  
- Bounded correction capacity under operational load  
- Recursive feedback loops between model outputs and training environments  

---

## Core Thesis

> AI systems do not primarily fail because they are misaligned with human intent.  
> They fail because they are correctly aligned to incentive structures that are themselves misaligned with long-term system stability.

Alignment under drift describes the condition where optimisation remains locally correct while global system stability degrades over time.

---

## Formal Stability Condition

Let:

- **D(t)** = system drift (accumulated irreversible optimisation error relative to long-horizon stability space)  
- **C(t)** = correction capacity (effective ability to detect and reverse drift, adjusted for independence constraints)  
- **L(t)** = operational load (system complexity and autonomy depth)

### Stability condition:
C(t) > D(t) + L(t)
### Instability regime:

When drift outpaces correction capacity under sustained load, systems remain operationally functional while becoming structurally unrecoverable.

## Key Contributions

### 1. Correction Embedding Principle
Correction mechanisms embedded within the same incentive structure as the systems they regulate asymptotically converge toward the system’s drift trajectory.

### 2. Drift-Based Alignment Model
Alignment is reframed as a control stability problem under irreversible state accumulation.

### 3. Recursive Convergence Dynamics
Training pipelines that incorporate model-generated data induce convergence toward institutional objective spaces over time.

### 4. Structural Asymmetry
Performance optimisation pressures and long-term stability requirements are structurally opposed under temporal compression.

---

## Primary Failure Modes

### Drift Saturation Failure
Correction mechanisms remain active while failing to reduce underlying drift trajectory.

### Correction Capture Failure
Audit and oversight systems converge toward the same objective distortion as the system being corrected.

### Recursive Convergence Lock
Objective space narrows through recursive training feedback loops, reducing out-of-frame reasoning capacity.

## Structural Implications

CEP implies that alignment is not primarily an interpretability or reward modelling problem.

Instead, it is a **systems architecture problem** requiring:

- Independent correction layers  
- Architecturally enforced reversibility constraints  
- Baseline state preservation mechanisms  
- Separation between optimisation systems and audit systems
